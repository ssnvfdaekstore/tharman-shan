Tharman Shanmugaratnam: “The new digital bank licenses mark the next chapter in Singapore’s banking liberalization journey…”

by Naomi Abneyupdated on November 4, 2019November 4, 2019Leave a Comment on Tharman Shanmugaratnam: “The new digital bank licenses mark the next chapter in Singapore’s banking liberalization journey…”

As five new digital bank licenses are to be authorized to any player who can add value to the economy, Singapore’s banking system is now in its new chapter. The new digital banks to be issued will be an addition to any digital banks set up by local banking groups.

“The new digital bank licenses mark the next chapter in Singapore’s banking liberalization journey… We welcome firms with innovative value propositions to apply for the new digital bank licenses, even if they have not yet established a track record in banking,” said Senior Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam.

Monetary Authority Singapore (MAS) Senior Minister Tharman Shanmugaratnam has already anticipated invitations in August and will provide more information on the conditions and requirements for the applications.

There are also two digital full bank licenses to be issued by MAS to companies in Singapore, but foreign companies are also eligible.

“We are starting with two digital full bank licenses so as not to fragment Singapore’s small domestic retail banking market. We are also restricting the new licenses to players anchored in Singapore for two reasons.

“First, so that we maintain a strong local core in our banking system. Second, to avoid an unintended unilateral liberalization of our full bank regime as a result of our WTO (World Trade Organisation) commitments.” 

That’s what Mr. Tharman said at the annual dinner of the Banks Association in Singapore. Digital banks have shown tremendous potential to reduce expenses in the provision of financial services.

“MAS will not allow value-destructive competition to the detriment of long-term financial system stability. We will also put in place safeguards to protect depositors, mitigate the risk of untested business models and minimize costs to the financial system in the event of a failure. We will also ensure that digital banks will compete with incumbents on a level playing field,” Mr. Tharman added.

Digitalization has led to a fundamental rethink of the position of banks. The idea is to use technology to better serve clients while maintaining a good local anchor and trust in the banking system.
